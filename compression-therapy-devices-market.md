<p><p>Compression therapy devices are used to improve blood circulation and reduce swelling in patients with circulatory issues. Static compression therapy devices apply consistent pressure to the affected area using compression garments, while dynamic compression therapy devices apply intermittent pressure to promote lymphatic drainage. Static compression therapy is commonly used for conditions like venous insufficiency, while dynamic compression therapy is often used for lymphedema and sports injury recovery. Both types of devices aim to enhance circulation and speed up the healing process in patients with circulation problems.</p></p>
