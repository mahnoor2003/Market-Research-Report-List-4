<p><p>Ureteral catheters are medical devices used to drain urine from the bladder. There are two main types of ureteral catheters: intermittent catheters and Foley catheters. Intermittent catheters are inserted into the bladder only when needed and then removed, typically used for short-term drainage. Foley catheters are indwelling and remain in the bladder for a longer period of time, held in place by a small balloon. Both types of catheters serve the purpose of draining urine from the bladder effectively.</p></p>
