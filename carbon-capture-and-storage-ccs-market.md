<p><p>Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS) is a technology designed to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by capturing carbon dioxide during the combustion process of fossil fuels and storing it underground. The three main types of CCS markets are Pre-Combustion Carbon Capture, which involves removing carbon dioxide before combustion; Oxy-Combustion Carbon Capture, which burns fuels in pure oxygen to produce a high-purity stream of carbon dioxide for storage; and Post-Combustion Carbon Capture, which captures carbon dioxide from the exhaust gases after combustion.</p></p>
