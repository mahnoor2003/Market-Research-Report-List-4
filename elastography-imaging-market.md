<p><p>Elastography imaging is a non-invasive technique used to measure tissue stiffness, allowing for the diagnosis and monitoring of various medical conditions. There are two main types of elastography imaging: ultrasound elastography and magnetic resonance elastography. </p><p>Ultrasound elastography uses high-frequency sound waves to assess tissue stiffness, particularly in the liver, breast, and prostate. Magnetic resonance elastography combines MRI technology with mechanical waves to provide detailed images of tissue elasticity. Both techniques offer valuable insights for physicians in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases.</p></p>
