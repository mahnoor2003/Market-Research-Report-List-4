<p><p>Woodworking fasteners can be categorized into two main types: threaded fasteners and non-threaded fasteners. Threaded fasteners, such as screws and bolts, have helical ridges that grip the material they are being fastened to, providing a secure hold. Non-threaded fasteners, like nails and staples, rely on friction or other mechanisms to hold materials together. Both types of fasteners play a crucial role in woodworking projects, with each offering unique benefits and applications depending on the specific needs of the project.</p></p>
