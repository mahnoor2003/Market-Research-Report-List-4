<p><p>Endotracheal tubes are classified into two main types based on the route of insertion: Orotracheal intubation and nasotracheal intubation. In orotracheal intubation, the tube is inserted through the mouth and into the trachea, while in nasotracheal intubation, the tube is inserted through the nose and into the trachea. Both types of endotracheal tubes are coated with materials such as polyvinyl chloride to reduce friction and improve patient comfort during intubation procedures.</p></p>
