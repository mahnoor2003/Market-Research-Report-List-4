<p><p>Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC) filters are classified into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I filters are designed to reduce electromagnetic interference emitted by electronic devices, ensuring compliance with regulatory standards. They are commonly used in consumer electronics and household appliances. On the other hand, Type II filters are used to protect sensitive electronic equipment from external sources of electromagnetic interference. They are typically found in industrial applications, medical devices, and telecommunications equipment. Both types of EMC filters play a crucial role in ensuring optimal performance and safety of electronic systems.</p></p>
