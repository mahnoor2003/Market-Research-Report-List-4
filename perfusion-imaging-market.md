<p><p>Perfusion imaging is a diagnostic technique that evaluates blood flow to tissues and organs in the body. The three main types of perfusion imaging market include Computed Tomography (CT Scan), Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), and Nuclear Medicine. CT perfusion imaging uses X-rays to create detailed images of blood flow. MRI perfusion imaging uses magnetic fields and radio waves to produce high-resolution images. Nuclear medicine perfusion imaging involves the injection of radioactive tracers to track blood flow patterns. Each type has its own strengths and limitations in assessing perfusion.</p></p>
