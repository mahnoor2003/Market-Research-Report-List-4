<p><p>PVDF binders for batteries are commonly produced through two main processes: emulsion polymerization and suspension polymerization. In emulsion polymerization, PVDF is dissolved in water and stabilized with surfactants to form small droplets that polymerize to create a uniform binder. Suspension polymerization involves dispersing PVDF particles in water with the help of stabilizers, where they polymerize to form the binder. Both processes offer advantages in terms of binder quality, efficiency, and consistency for use in battery applications.</p></p>
