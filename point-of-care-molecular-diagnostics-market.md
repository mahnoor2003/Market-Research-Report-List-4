<p><p>Point-of-care molecular diagnostics refer to the testing of patient samples for disease markers at the point of care, allowing for rapid and accurate diagnosis. There are four main types of technologies used in point-of-care molecular diagnostics: PCR-based, Genetic Sequencing-based, Hybridization-based, and Microarray-based. PCR-based tests detect DNA/RNA using polymerase chain reaction; Genetic Sequencing-based tests analyze the genetic code of the sample; Hybridization-based tests detect specific DNA/RNA sequences using complementary probes; and Microarray-based tests analyze multiple genes simultaneously on a biochip.</p></p>
