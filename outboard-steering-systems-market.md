<p><p>Outboard steering systems typically fall into three main types: electronic power steering, hydraulic steering, and mechanical steering. </p><p>Electronic power steering systems utilize sensors and an electric motor to assist with steering, providing a smoother and more responsive driving experience. </p><p>Hydraulic steering systems use hydraulic fluid to transfer steering inputs from the helm to the outboard motor, offering precise control and ease of maneuverability. </p><p>Mechanical steering systems rely on cables and pulleys to transmit steering movements, offering reliable performance at a lower cost.</p></p>
