<p><ul><li>Positron Emission Tomography</li><li>Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography</li><li>Planar Scintigraphy Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nuclear molecular imaging systems are used for detailed imaging of the human body for diagnosing various medical conditions. The market is segmented into three main types - Positron Emission Tomography (PET), Single-photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT), and Planar Scintigraphy Systems. PET systems use radioactive substances to create 3D images, while SPECT systems use gamma rays to produce detailed images. Planar scintigraphy systems capture 2D images of the body's organs and tissues. These imaging systems play a crucial role in the diagnosis and treatment of various diseases.</p></p>
