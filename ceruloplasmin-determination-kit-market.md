<p><p>Ceruloplasmin determination kits use different methods to measure the levels of ceruloplasmin in a sample. Nephelometry measures the amount of light scattered by particles in the sample, providing a highly sensitive and accurate measurement. Immunoturbidimetry measures the amount of light absorbed by the particles in the sample, providing a quick and cost-effective measurement. Both methods are commonly used in clinical laboratories for diagnosing conditions related to ceruloplasmin levels and monitoring treatment effectiveness.</p></p>
