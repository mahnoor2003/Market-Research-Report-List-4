<p><ul><li>Cardioid Polar Pattern</li><li>Supercardioid or Hypercardioid Polar Pattern</li><li>Omnidirectional Polar Pattern</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Boundary microphones are designed to be placed on a surface, such as a table or floor, to pick up sound evenly from all directions. The market for boundary microphones is segmented into three types based on polar patterns: Cardioid Polar Pattern, which is most sensitive to sound in front of the microphone; Supercardioid or Hypercardioid Polar Pattern, which have narrower pickup patterns for reduced ambient noise; and Omnidirectional Polar Pattern, which captures sound from all directions equally. These different types cater to various audio recording needs and preferences.</p></p>
